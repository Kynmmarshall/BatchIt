1. Executive Summary
Batchit is a mobile-first collaborative bulk purchasing platform that solves a common consumer
pain point: purchasing minimums that exceed individual needs. By connecting consumers who
want to buy portions of a bulk product, Batchit allows users to split the cost and quantity,
reducing waste and saving money.


The platform is centered around Providers (retailers, wholesalers, or suppliers) who list their
products. Customers find products they need, create or join Batches — group purchase
requests — and coordinate purchases through the provider. This provider-centric model
removes the challenge of customer-to-customer coordination across regions and retailer
preferences.


  Core Value Proposition
  Batchit reduces per-unit purchase cost and product waste by enabling customers to pool demand.
  Providers gain access to aggregated bulk orders, improving their sales volume. Everyone wins.




2. Problem Statement
2.1 The Core Problem
Many consumer goods are sold in bulk quantities that exceed what a single buyer needs. This
creates several friction points:
   •   A customer needs 15 toilet rolls but the pack contains 20 — they either overspend or go
       without.
   •   Bulk packs offer lower per-unit prices, but individuals cannot always take advantage of
       that pricing.
   •   Product waste increases when buyers are forced to purchase more than required.



2.2 Problems with the Original Peer-to-Peer Model
An initial concept of connecting customers directly with each other faced the following
challenges:

 Problem                          Impact

 Hard to find others              Customers wanting to split the same product at the same time are
                                  hard to match.

 Geographic barriers              Customers from different regions cannot easily coordinate a shared
                                  purchase.
 Retailer mismatch                  Customer A may prefer Retailer X while Customer B prefers
                                    Retailer Y — making a split impossible.

 Trust issues                       Peer-to-peer money handling raises concerns about fraud and
                                    reliability.
 Scalability                        Matching individual customers for every purchase is complex and
                                    operationally heavy.




2.3 The Shifted Solution: Provider-Centric Model
Batchit resolved these issues by pivoting to a provider-centric approach. Instead of matching
customers to each other, everything revolves around a Provider — a business, retailer, or
supplier who lists their goods on the platform. This means:
   •      All customers shopping at the same provider can join the same batch.
   •      Geographic friction is eliminated since the provider handles logistics.
   •      The provider fulfills the bulk order and manages delivery or pickup.
   •      Batchit facilitates the grouping and coordination, not the money transfer between users.

3.2 How a Batch Works
A Batch is the fundamental unit of activity on Batchit. The lifecycle is as follows:
   1. A customer identifies a product they want to purchase partially (e.g., 15 of 20 toilet rolls).
   2. The customer creates a Batch, specifying the product, their desired quantity, and the
      provider.
   3. The Batch is shared with the provider's subscriber pool. Other customers who want the
      remaining quantity can join.
   4. Once the Batch is fully filled (total quantity matches the pack size), the batch is sent to
      the provider.
   5. The provider fulfills the order in bulk. Each participant receives their purchased portion.


  Listening & Sending — Dual Subscription Benefit
  When subscribed to a provider, a customer can both SEND new batches to that provider AND LISTEN
  for batches created by other customers at that provider — so they are automatically notified and can
  join batches they did not create.




4. User Flows
4.1 Customer Onboarding Flow
  New User Journey

   6. App Launch — Splash screen with Batchit branding.
   7. Onboarding Screens — 3–5 introductory slides explaining the concept: what a batch is,
       how providers work, and how to save money.
   8. Questionnaire — Collects user preferences (product categories of interest, shopping
       frequency, preferred regions, budget range). Used to power recommendations.
   9. Account Creation — User enters name, email/phone, and password. Optionally continue
       with Google/Apple.
   10. Login — Authenticated session established.
   11. Home Screen — User lands on the main dashboard. Profile can be completed later via
       the Profile section.



4.2 Returning User Flow
   12. App Launch — Automatic login if session is active.
   13. Home Screen — Feed of recommended batches and popular providers is shown.
   14. User browses, searches, creates, or joins batches.
   15. Notifications alert user to batch activity.



4.3 Creating a Batch (Customer)
   16. From the Home Screen or Provider page, tap 'Create Batch'.
   17. Select product from the provider's catalogue.
   18. Specify desired quantity (must be less than the full pack quantity).
   19. Choose which subscribed providers to send the batch to.
   20. Optionally add a note or deadline for the batch.
   21. Batch is published. Subscribed customers at the chosen provider are notified.



4.4 Joining a Batch (Customer)
   22. User receives notification of a new batch at a subscribed provider, OR discovers a batch
       via search.
   23. User reviews batch details: product, quantity needed, quantity available, participants.
   24. User taps 'Join Batch' and specifies how many units they want.
   25. If the batch is now fully filled, it moves to fulfillment.
   26. If not yet full, the batch stays open for more joiners.



4.5 Provider Flow (High-Level)
While the provider side requires its own detailed specification, the high-level flow is:
   27. Provider registers and creates a business profile.
   28. Provider lists products: name, description, pack size, unit price, available stock.
   29. Customers subscribe to the provider.
   30. Provider receives incoming batches (group purchase requests).
   31. Provider reviews, confirms, and fulfills the batch order.
   32. Provider can also initiate special offers or bulk deals that appear as promoted batches.

6. Subscriptions & Provider Relationships
6.1 Subscribing to a Provider
Customers subscribe to providers they frequently shop at or are interested in. The subscription
grants two capabilities:

 Capability                        What It Means

 LISTEN                            When any other customer creates a batch at this provider, the
                                   subscribed customer is automatically notified and can join that
                                   batch.

 SEND                              When the subscribed customer creates a new batch, they can
                                   choose to broadcast it to this provider so other subscribers are
                                   notified.




6.2 Subscription Management
   •     Customers can subscribe and unsubscribe from providers at any time via the Provider
         Profile or the Profile > Subscriptions section.
   •     Customers can control notification preferences per provider (e.g., notify for all new
         batches vs. only for specific product categories).
   •     Popular and recommended providers are surfaced on the Home Screen to encourage
         new subscriptions.

9. System Architecture
9.1 Architecture Overview
Batchit follows a client-server architecture with the following major components:

 Component                        Responsibility
Mobile App (Customer)          React Native or Flutter app for iOS and Android. Consumes the
                               REST/GraphQL API.

Mobile App (Provider)          Separate provider-facing app or web dashboard. Manages product
                               listings and incoming batches.
API Gateway                    Entry point for all client requests. Handles authentication, rate
                               limiting, and routing.

Auth Service                   JWT-based authentication. Supports email/password and OAuth
                               (Google, Apple).
User Service                   Manages customer and provider profiles, questionnaire data,
                               preferences.

Batch Service                  Core business logic — creating, joining, filling, expiring batches.

Notification Service           Sends push notifications (FCM/APNs) and manages in-app
                               notification feeds.

Subscription Service           Manages customer-provider subscription relationships.

Search Service                 Full-text search across batches and providers. Powered by
                               Elasticsearch or Algolia.

Recommendation Engine          Generates personalized provider and batch recommendations
                               based on questionnaire data and activity.

Database                       PostgreSQL for relational data. Redis for caching and real-time
                               batch state.

File Storage                   AWS S3 or equivalent for product images, provider logos, and
                               profile photos.
Push Notification Provider     Firebase Cloud Messaging (Android) + APNs (iOS).

10. Security & Privacy Considerations
  •     All API communication over HTTPS/TLS 1.3.
  •     Passwords hashed with bcrypt (minimum 12 rounds), but for now nothing just simple
        hashing in django
  •     JWT tokens with short expiry (15 minutes) and refresh token rotation.
  •     Customer personal data encrypted at rest (AES-256).
  •     GDPR / data privacy compliance — users can request data deletion.
  •     Provider verification process to prevent fraudulent listings.
  •     Rate limiting on all public-facing endpoints to prevent abuse.
  •     Financial transactions handled by a certified payment processor (Stripe, Flutterwave, or
        equivalent) — Batchit does not store card data, but for now nothing.
